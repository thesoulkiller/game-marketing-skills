EVAL NOTES — Baseline (No Skill) — eval-1-new-game
=======================================================

Task: Create a game marketing context document for a new pre-launch mobile game.
Condition: No skill file loaded. General AI assistant behavior only.

--- APPROACH ---

Without a skill to guide the structure, I relied on general knowledge of:
- Mobile game marketing best practices (F2P genre norms)
- Common marketing context document structures used in game studios
- Industry benchmarks for casual/mid-core fantasy games
- Pre-launch vs. soft launch vs. global launch marketing phases

I interpreted "game marketing context" as a foundational document that captures the product, audience, positioning, channels, messaging, and goals — similar to what a marketing lead would prepare before a launch campaign kicks off.

--- STRUCTURE DECISIONS ---

Chose to organize the document into 11 sections:
1. Product overview — core facts about the game
2. Target audience — demographic + psychographic + player archetypes
3. Value proposition — the "why this game" pitch
4. Competitive landscape — direct and adjacent competitors, differentiation
5. Marketing objectives by phase — pre-launch, soft launch, global
6. Key channels — organic, paid, influencer, ASO
7. Messaging framework — primary/supporting messages + tone
8. Pre-registration strategy — tactics and goals
9. Content pillars — recurring content themes
10. KPIs — organized by phase
11. Assumptions — documenting gaps where user input was needed

--- ASSUMPTIONS MADE ---

Since the user provided only genre, business model, target demo, and timeline, I filled in:
- Placeholder game name ("Realm Forge")
- Standard F2P monetization model (gems, battle pass, cosmetics)
- Colorful/vibrant fantasy art style assumed (most common for this target demo)
- Puzzle mechanic assumed to be match-3 or similar (most common in mobile)
- Standard soft launch markets (Canada, Australia, Philippines, Nordics)
- Pre-registration goal of 50K-100K (typical for mid-size launch)
- LTV:CAC target of 3:1 at 180 days (industry norm for sustainable F2P)
- Day 7 retention target of 25%+ (mid-core casual hybrid benchmark)

--- WHAT A SKILL WOULD LIKELY IMPROVE ---

A dedicated skill would likely:
- Prompt for specific missing inputs before generating (game name, art style, puzzle type, budget, team size)
- Follow a standardized template structure used specifically for game marketing contexts
- Include game-specific frameworks (e.g., FTUE conversion funnel, genre-specific UA creative formulas)
- Reference specific tools relevant to game marketing (Appsflyer, Sensor Tower, GameAnalytics, etc.)
- Separate mobile game UA strategy more granularly (rewarded video, playables, etc.)
- Include a soft launch decision framework (when to graduate to global)
- Potentially generate a one-page positioning brief as a separate artifact

--- QUALITY SELF-ASSESSMENT ---

Strengths:
- Comprehensive coverage of pre-launch marketing phases
- Audience archetypes add depth beyond demographics
- KPIs organized by phase are immediately actionable
- Competitive framing is accurate for 2024-2026 mobile market

Weaknesses:
- No game name — limits memorability and specificity
- Assumptions around puzzle mechanic and art style could be wrong
- Budget and team context missing — channel priorities may not fit reality
- No mention of specific tools/platforms used for UA tracking (MMP, analytics)
- Messaging could be sharper with actual game name and confirmed mechanics
- Missing: localization strategy, platform-specific ASO details, influencer tier breakdown with rates

--- CONCLUSION ---

The output is a reasonable starting point for a pre-launch mobile game marketing context. It covers the essential pillars a marketing team would need to align on. However, it would benefit significantly from: (1) a skill that prompts for the right inputs systematically, and (2) access to current industry benchmarks and game-specific tooling knowledge. A skilled agent would likely produce a more structured, game-specific, and actionable output with fewer assumptions.
