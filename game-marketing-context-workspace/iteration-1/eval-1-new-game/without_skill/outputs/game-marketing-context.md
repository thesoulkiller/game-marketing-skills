## Target Audience

**Primary Demographic:**
- Age: 18–35
- Gender: Mixed, slight male skew typical for city-builders and puzzle-battlers
- Casual to mid-core players: engage daily in short sessions (5–15 min) but invest in progression

**Psychographic Profile:**
- Enjoy fantasy aesthetics (dragons, kingdoms, magic)
- Motivated by building, progression, and collection
- Competitive enough to engage with PvP/leaderboard elements but not hardcore
- Like bite-sized challenges with visible rewards
- Tend to play before bed, during commute, or in downtime

**Player Archetypes:**
- The Builder: drawn to city construction, optimization, and visual growth
- The Puzzle Solver: motivated by puzzle challenge and skill expression
- The Collector: driven by unlocking heroes, buildings, cosmetics
- The Competitor: engaged by leaderboards, guilds, limited-time events

---

## Core Value Proposition

"Grow your fantasy kingdom through the power of puzzle mastery — every battle you win builds your empire."

The game fuses two proven casual/mid-core genres:
1. **City-builder loop**: persistent base that grows over time, visual progression, resource management
2. **Puzzle battle loop**: skill-based matches that feel rewarding, not purely luck-based

This hybrid gives players two hooks: the city-builder satisfies long-term investment and the puzzle battles provide daily active engagement.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct Competitors (Hybrid puzzle + builder):**
- Merge Mansion — merge puzzle + narrative progression
- Royal Match — match-3 puzzle + castle renovation
- Puzzles & Survival — puzzle battle + strategy base
- Evony: The King's Return — city-builder + battle, mobile

**Adjacent Competitors:**
- Clash of Clans / Clash Royale — fantasy battle/builder
- Homescapes / Gardenscapes — puzzle + meta-game renovation
- Rise of Kingdoms — city-builder, fantasy, mid-core

**Differentiation Opportunities:**
- Deeper city-builder depth than Royal Match-style games
- More skill-expressive puzzle mechanics than merge games
- Fantasy IP distinctiveness vs. renovation-themed competitors
- Mid-core depth that retains players longer than pure casual puzzle games

## Marketing Objectives (Pre-Launch to Soft Launch)

### Pre-Launch (Now → Month 2)
- Build awareness among target audience on TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube Shorts
- Grow pre-registration list (App Store / Google Play pre-reg campaigns)
- Establish social media presence and community (Discord, Reddit, Instagram)
- Create content assets: gameplay clips, cinematic trailers, puzzle highlight reels

### Soft Launch (Month 3)
- Acquire initial installs in test markets (typically Canada, Australia, Philippines, Nordic countries)
- Gather data on Day 1/7/30 retention, ARPU, LTV proxies
- Test creative performance across paid UA channels (Meta, Google UAC, TikTok Ads)
- Validate monetization funnels before scaling spend

### Global Launch (Post-Soft Launch)
- Scale paid UA based on validated LTV/CAC ratios
- Drive App Store / Google Play featuring consideration
- Influencer and content creator partnerships (mid-tier, genre-relevant)

## Key Marketing Channels

### Organic / Community
- **TikTok**: Short gameplay clips, puzzle challenge videos, "satisfying city-build" content
- **Instagram**: City aesthetics, visual progression screenshots, Reels
- **YouTube**: Gameplay trailers, dev diaries, puzzle highlights
- **Discord**: Community server for early adopters, beta testers, feedback
- **Reddit**: Engagement in r/gachagaming, r/AndroidGaming, r/iosgaming, r/citybuildinggames

### Paid UA (Post-Soft Launch Primarily)
- **Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)**: Playable ads, video creatives showing puzzle gameplay + city progression
- **Google UAC**: App campaign across Search, YouTube, Display, Play Store
- **TikTok Ads**: Spark Ads boosting organic content, in-feed video ads
- **AppLovin / ironSource**: Rewarded and interstitial placements in complementary games

### Influencer / Content Creators
- Mid-tier mobile gaming creators (100K–2M YouTube/TikTok)
- Genre: mobile RPG, city-builder, puzzle game channels
- Strategy: sponsored first-looks, "let's play" puzzle challenge content

### App Store Optimization (ASO)
- Keywords: fantasy city builder, kingdom puzzle, merge battle, castle puzzle game
- A/B test icon (city panorama vs. action/battle moment)
- Screenshots: lead with puzzle gameplay + city transformation split-screen

## Messaging Framework

### Primary Message
"Build your kingdom. Win your battles."

### Supporting Messages
- "Every puzzle solved brings your kingdom one step closer to glory"
- "A city-builder with a brain — master the puzzle, rule the realm"
- "Casual fun. Epic kingdoms."

### Tone of Voice
- Adventurous and aspirational
- Accessible, not intimidating
- Rewarding and celebratory
- Avoid overly dark/grimdark tone — fantasy should feel heroic and fun

## Pre-Registration Strategy

**Goal:** 50,000–100,000 pre-registrations before soft launch (aspirational benchmark)

**Tactics:**
- Enable pre-registration on Google Play and App Store as soon as eligible
- Offer pre-reg reward (exclusive cosmetic, starter currency pack, hero/unit)
- Promote pre-reg link across all organic channels
- Run lightweight paid campaigns on Meta and TikTok to drive pre-reg sign-ups
- Cross-promote in genre-adjacent communities

4. **Community & Player Stories** — Leaderboard highlights, player kingdom showcases

## KPIs to Track

### Pre-Launch
- Pre-registration count
- Social follower growth (TikTok, Instagram, Discord)
- Content engagement rate (views, shares, saves)
- Email/community list size

### Soft Launch
- Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 30 retention
- Install volume in test markets
- Cost Per Install (CPI) by channel
- ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) at Day 30
- Conversion rate: install → first purchase
- Creative performance (CTR, IPM by ad variant)

### Global Launch
- LTV:CAC ratio (target >3:1 at 180 days)
- Day 7 retention (target: >25% for this genre)
- App Store rating (target: 4.4+)
- ROAS by channel at 7/14/30 days
