## Target Audience

### Original Estimate
- **Age Range**: 18–35
- **Gender**: Broad male skew

### Updated (based on campaign data)
- **Age Range**: 25–40 ← revised upward based on Meta campaign demographic breakdown
- **Gender**: Male-dominant (skew confirmed; female audience minimal in Wave 1)
- **Psychographic**: Strategy enthusiasts, mid-core RPG/tactics fans, competitive PvP players
- **Geos**: United States, Germany, United Kingdom

> **Note**: The 18–24 cohort underperformed in both volume and conversion. The 25–40 bracket drove the bulk of installs and showed higher engagement. Recommend shifting budget and creative targeting to 25–40 male as the primary ICP.

## UA / Performance Targets vs. Actuals

| Metric | Target | Actual (Wave 1) | Delta | Status |
|--------|--------|-----------------|-------|--------|
| CPI (US) | $1.50 | $1.80 | +$0.30 | Above target |
| CPI (DE) | $1.00 | $0.95 | -$0.05 | On target |
| D7 Retention | 22% | 18% | -4pp | Below target |

### CPI Analysis
- **US**: $1.80 actual vs. $1.50 target — 20% above benchmark. Likely driven by broad 18–35 targeting inflating CPM. Recommend tightening to 25–40 male and testing lookalikes off paying users once cohort matures.
- **DE**: $0.95 actual vs. $1.00 target — slightly under budget, performing well. Germany is an efficient market for this genre. Maintain or modestly scale.

### Retention Analysis
- **D7 at 18%** is 4 percentage points below the 22% target — a meaningful gap.
- Possible causes: onboarding friction, mismatch between ad creative promise and in-game early experience, or audience quality issues from broad targeting.
- **Recommended actions**:
  1. Audit D1 → D3 funnel for drop-off points (check Adjust event funnels)
  2. Review whether ad creative sets accurate expectations (avoid over-promising in hooks)
  3. Test a narrower, higher-intent audience (e.g., lookalikes from D7+ retained users once available)
  4. Consider a D3 push notification re-engagement for at-risk churners
