## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Players love | Falls short | Our edge |
|------------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Royal Match (Dream Games) | Extremely polished puzzle loop, low friction, strong narrative framing (King Robert character) | City-building is surface-level decoration only; no strategic depth | Deeper kingdom-building layer gives progression-hungry players more to invest in; puzzle directly advances the city |
| Township (Playrix) | Strong city-builder and farming loop, social features, high polish | Puzzle component (match-3) feels disconnected from city building; no puzzle skill expression | Tighter integration — puzzle victory directly determines what gets built; more satisfying causal link |
| Merge Dragons / Merge Magic (Gram Games) | Satisfying merge mechanic, fantasy theme, cozy pacing, low time pressure | Merge loop can feel repetitive over time; limited direct skill agency per session | Active puzzle battles give players skill expression and direct agency every session |

**Most convertible competitor audience:** Royal Match players who want more strategic depth in their kingdom progression; Township players who want more active gameplay when waiting on build timers.
